Data Portrait

Another sketch I am quite happy with! In this assignment, we were to collect some sort of data, and visualize it in any way we found to be appropriate. I wanted to lean into the visualization aspect of this, and tell a story that almost completely hid the fact that the sketch was inspired by data. My data portrait pertained to me, but wasn't really my data. Rather, I tracked how often my two cats (1) came to me for pets and (2) came just to hang out with me: in my room, on my bed, or just in my general vicinity. I did the pets break down per day (this correlates to how open the eyes are; the more closed the eyes, the more they came for pets). I combined the hanging out data as a total of all days combined (on the first mouse click, the eyes change size depending on this number). What I love about this final sketch is that it tells a story completely visually, and without knowing exactly what data is collected and how it correlates to the interactivity of the sketch itself, it seems ambiguous and hidden. This idea of "hidden" data in our current cultural climate is typically wrought with fear of government surveillance. I like that I was able to subvert this norm, creating a pretty cute and wholesome display of data.
